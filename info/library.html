üìñ AI Glossary

Essential terms, explained simply. Bookmark this page ‚Äî you'll come back to it.

LLM (Large Language Model)

An AI trained on vast text data that can generate, summarize, and reason about language. GPT-4, Claude, and Llama are examples.

Token

A chunk of text (roughly ¬æ of a word). LLMs process text as tokens, and pricing is usually per-token.

Prompt

The input you give an AI. The quality of your prompt directly shapes the quality of the output.

Hallucination

When an AI generates plausible-sounding but factually incorrect information. Always verify critical claims.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

A technique that feeds relevant documents to an AI before generating, dramatically improving accuracy and relevance.

Fine-Tuning

Training a pre-existing model on your specific data to specialize its behavior for your use case.

Embedding

A numerical representation of text that captures its meaning. Used for search, similarity, and clustering.

Temperature

A parameter controlling randomness. Low temperature = focused and predictable. High = creative and varied.

Context Window

The maximum amount of text an AI can "see" at once. Larger windows allow longer conversations and documents.

Agent

An AI system that can take actions, use tools, and make decisions ‚Äî not just generate text.
